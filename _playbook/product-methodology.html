Vets.gov combines human-centered design with agile development to deliver products grounded in customer need and supported through cycles of continuous improvement.

This methodology is intended for teams designing new products for vets.gov and for those migrating existing tools and content from VA websites. The framework, while not prescriptive, is a best practice model that aligns human-centered design with the vets.gov development and migration process. The activities of cross-functional teams are detailed out by phase as well as specific VA stakeholder engagement points with the Vets.gov

  2. Kickoff

    The kickoff meeting is conducted after completion of project definition and brings together the entire project team. It creates alignment and shared understanding of the project’s goals, scope, milestones, roles and responsibilities. It builds on the project definition phase by eliciting known business requirements, user needs and technical platform dependencies.

    Learn about Kickoff [Link]

  3. Discovery

    Discovery validates and expands upon the initial project definition. Deeper understanding is developed through design research focused on business and customer needs. Technical investigation also happens in this phase. These activities serve as the foundation for product concept exploration.

    Learn about Discovery

    VA Stakeholder Engagement
    1. Participate in working sessions to provide business requirements, validate design concepts, and provide feedback to vets.gov team as needed.
    2. Provide requested documentation and timely responses to questions as requested.
    3. Provide introductions / access to end users as available.
    4. Provide any available feedback or data about end users
    5. Develop launch communication plan
    6. Provide input for sunset strategy for systems and content
    Engineering
    1. Identify all required data elements.
    2. Identify data collection needs and data-driven goals. (For example, tracking completion rates for a form, or top search terms.)
    3. Identify related systems. Plan for rebuild, or integration. (For example sending an electronic disability claim.)
    4. Define system architecture including data, security, integrations and services
    Search
    1. Identify current user search terms on Google, VA.gov
    2. Identify initial keyword list
    Content
    1. Define goals for content. What do users need?
    2. Review existing content, online and off. What can be incorporated? What needs to be newly created?
    3. Define feature set – the content elements that will be included in this release.
    4. Use Vets.gov Style Guide in creating new content and revising any that’s reused.
    Design Research
    1. Review existing research to determine how much additional research is required.
    2. Create a research plan
    3. Conduct research
    4. Synthesize findings
    5. Document findings
    6. Share with larger core vets.gov team and with the business partner team

  3. Sunset

     Existing VA products will need to be retired after they are re-designed and brought into the platform. Care should be taken to communicate these changes to users and to help them transition as needed.

    Learn about Sunset [Link]

    Output
    • Product and/or content retirement
    Lead
    • Project Team POC
    • VA Product Lead
    • VA Business Owner
    Participants
    • Business Product Owner
    • VA Content Writers
    • Engineering
    Team activities
    1. Identify how user needs will be served after product sunset to ensure there is no unintentional degradation of service
    2. Clearly communicate changes on legacy websites and vets.Gov (if applicable)
    3. Implement redirects where needed
    4. Protect existing data
    5. Retire legacy content and systems
    6. Monitor call center and help desk to support any issues immediately following retirements
