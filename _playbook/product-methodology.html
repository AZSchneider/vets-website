Vets.gov combines human centered design with agile development to deliver products grounded in customer need and supported through cycles of continuous improvement.

Discover

  1. Project definition

    An outline of the project’s key components. This should include a description of the current environment.

    Activities

    • Define project goals
    • Define scope
    • Create timeline
    • Identify resources, allocations and budget
    • Define and assign roles
    • Define Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
    • Create a project plan
    • Consult with vets.gov team for approval
    Owner:
    • Business Owner (team)
    Output:
    • Project plan

  2. Kickoff

    The kickoff meeting gathers the entire project team. It creates alignment and shared understanding of the project’s goals and scope.

    Activities
    • Assemble entire team. (Invite subject matter experts, when relevant.)
    • Review project goals and definitions. Adjust as needed.
    • Review known business requirements.
    • Review known user needs.
    Owners:
    • Business Owner and vets.gov team
      • Output:
      • Kickoff meeting

  3. Discovery

    Discovery validates and expands upon the initial project definition. Deeper understanding is developed through design research focused on business and customer needs. Technical investigation also happens in this phase. These serve as the foundation for product concept exploration.

    Engineering Activities:
    • Identify all required data elements.
    • Identify data collection needs and data-driven goals. (For example, tracking completion rates for a form, or top search terms.)
    • Identify related systems. Plan for rebuild, or integration. (For example sending an electronic disability claim.)
    Search
    • Identify current user search terms on Google, VA.gov
    • Identify initial keyword list
    Content Activities
    • Define goals for content. What do users need?
    • Review existing content, online and off. What can be incorporated? What needs to be newly created?
    • Define feature set – the content elements that will be included in this release.
    • Use Vets.gov Style Guide in creating new content and revising any that’s reused.
    Design Research Activities
    • Review existing research to determine how much additional research is required.
    • Create a research plan
    • Conduct research
    • Synthesize findings
    • Document findings
    • Share with larger core vets.gov team and with the business partner team
    Design Activities
    • Heuristic audit
    Owners
    • Engineering (data integration, back-end dev)
    • Content Strategist
    • Design researcher
    • UX Designer
    • Data analytics (what is this role?)
    Outputs
    • Discovery Report
    • Reccomendations
    • Priority Assessment
    • Gap Analysis
    • electronic disability claim.)

Design

  1. Alpha

     

    Alpha kicks off the iterative design and development process. Here, insights from the discovery phase inform product and concept prototypes. These are tested with real users to validate and refine the approach.

    Design Activities:
    • Design strategy
    • Brainstorming
    • Co-design
    • Experience Design
    • Visual Design mockups
    • Prototyping
    Content Activities
    • Fully written examples of each page type, beginning to end
    • Test original assumptions about content type and display
    • Refine as needed.
    Front-end Activities:
    • Prototyping
    • Front-end build
    Back-end Activities
    • Logical data flows mapped e.g.
    • Identify new data elements needed
    Testing Activities
    • Customer feedback sessions
    Owners
    • UX Designer
    • Visual Designer
    • Content Strategist
    • Information Architect
    • Front-End Developer
    • Back-End Developer
    Outputs
    • Working Prototype

  2. Beta

    Beta continues the iterative process, with increasing refinements moving much closer toward a fully functional live product with solid back-enintegration.

    Design Activities
    • Brainstorming
    • Co-design
    • Experience Design
    • Visual Design mockups
    • Prototyping
    Content Activities
    • Content complete and posted
    • Continue testing for clarity and user understanding
    • Final edit of site
    • Final copy edit of site
    • Signoff on subject content concurrence
    • Legal Concurrence
    Front-end Activities:
    • Prototyping
    • Front-end build
    Back-end Activities
    • Data Integration
    Testing Activities
    • Customer feedback sessions
    • Usability testing
    Owners
    • UX Designer
    • Visual Designer
    • Content Strategist
    • Information Architect
    • Front-End Developer
    • Back-End Developer
    Outputs
    • Working Prototype

  1. Pre-Flight

     

    The preflight check insures critical accessibility and transparency requirements have been addressed and that business sign-off has occurred before the product is public.

    Activities

    All pages tracked through Google Analytics

    508 compliance
    • Code is open-sourced
    • 100% passing code test coverage
    • Implementation of clear customer feedback mechanism
    Owners
    • Design Team
    • Engineering Team
    Outputs
    • Go live ready product version

  2. Go Live

     Go Live is the point at which a Vets.Gov product is pushed to the public. Products undergo a process of continuous improvement in response to  ongoing customer feedback and the addition of new capabilities.

    Activities
    • Launch
    • Continuous improvements
    • Assessment of success metrics/measurement
    Owners
    • Engineering Team
    • Design Team
    • Business Owners
    • Outputs
    • Public Product

  3. Sunset

     Vets.gov products may reach retirement due the introduction of new or improved tools or policy changes. Care should be taken to communicate these changes to users and to help them transition as needed. Existing VA products will need to be retired after they are re-designed and brought into the Harbor.

    Activities
    • Identify how user needs will be served after product sunset to ensure there is no unintentional degradation of service
    • Clearly communicate changes on Vets.Gov (if applicable)
    • Implement redirects where needed
    • Protect existing data
    Owners
    • Business Owner
    • Writer
    • Engineering
    Output
    • Product retirement
