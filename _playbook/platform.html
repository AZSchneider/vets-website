The Platform

The Vets.gov platform, code-named The Harbor, helps ensure customers and those building customer experiences have a consistent experience held to high standards, utilizing the best in design research and technology.

Services and Infrastructure

The Harbor provides central services, such as login, access to customer data, robust analytics, look/feel, design patterns, training, and toolkits. Within the Harbor, individual applications (“boats”) may dock and connect with the services they need. These apps are well-scoped and may be organized loosely into fleets for coordination purposes, e.g. “Employment Fleet” with boats for searching for jobs, posting a job-seeking profile, and translating military service into civilian skills.

The Harbor is built and maintained internally within VA (currently helmed by the VA Digital Service). Individual boats may be constructed by internal teams, contractors, and/or other resources (e.g. VERC, 18F), and will benefit from the Harbor infrastructure to deliver more and better functionality to customers, faster, without sacrificing quality, security, or privacy. A boat may be designed and sponsored by a business office, a VA administration, or in certain cases by the VE and/or DSE team.

The Harbor also has strong enforcement. You cannot enter the Harbor if your boat leaks or if you are a giant Carnival Cruise ship. You must regularly scrape barnacles off your bow and keep your paint fresh. You cannot moor a bicycle. In real terms, this means apps must be 508-compliant, adhere to automated test and code review standards, and that in some cases, a legacy system will simply not be able to make the cut.

Levels of Complexity

The Vets.gov platform will include features ranging from basic information to personalized applications. The framework below describes the range of offerings and the supporting architecture.

  • Level 1: Clear Information
    Features: None
    Hosting: Github
    FISMA: N/A
    Example: New Disability Claims content
  • Level 2: Basic Tools
    Features: Interactive forms
    Hosting: Amazon web services pilot (GSA IAA)
    FISMA: Low
    Example: Saved Veterans Employment Center information
  • Level 3: Account Linked Tools
    Features: Personalization
    Hosting: Amazon web services pilot (GSA IAA)
    FISMA: Moderate
    Example: Saved Veterans Employment Center information
  • Level 4: Sensitive Data Linked Tools
    Features: Integrated User Profile
    Hosting: TBD
